# The team behind SQ-GAN: A comprehensive academic investigation

**Style Quantization for Data-Efficient GAN Training (SQ-GAN), published at CVPR 2025, emerges from a tightly-knit research group at Sichuan University's College of Computer Science.** The five authors — Jian Wang, Xin Lan, Jizhe Zhou, Yuxin Tian, and Jiancheng Lv — form a clear academic hierarchy: two PhD students as co-first authors, an associate professor as direct supervisor, a senior PhD student as collaborator, and the dean of the school as principal investigator. Their two labs, DICALab and MachineILab, share an intellectual lineage rooted in neural network theory, and this paper represents the latest output of a sustained, multi-year push into data-efficient generative modeling that already yielded an ICML 2024 paper (MS³D) by the same core team.

This report serves as a "Private Investigator" role presentation for COMP547/447 Deep Unsupervised Learning at Koç University, taught by Prof. Aykut Erdem.

## 7. How SQ-GAN works and where it fits in the field

**The core problem:** When GANs train on limited data (e.g., 100–1000 images), the discriminator overfits, and the continuous latent space becomes too sparse to exploit effectively. Adjacent latent variables z and z+ε may generate images with wildly different realism, undermining consistency regularization.

**SQ-GAN's three innovations:**

First, **style space quantization** maps continuous latent variables z through StyleGAN's mapping network into the intermediate style space W, segments the resulting w into sub-vectors, and quantizes each to its nearest neighbor in a learnable codebook. This transforms the sparse continuous space into a compact discrete proxy space where each code controls distinct variation factors.

Second, a **knowledge-enhanced codebook initialization** uses CLIP's image encoder to extract features from training images, projects codebook codes into the same feature space, and aligns them via **optimal transport distance**. This injects semantic knowledge from a foundation model into the codebook before GAN training begins.

Third, a **quantization-based consistency regularization** ensures that nearby codes in the discrete space generate semantically similar images, making CR far more effective than in continuous space.

**Key baselines and related work:**

- **StyleGAN2-ADA** (Karras et al., NeurIPS 2020) is SQ-GAN's base architecture because its mapping network provides the disentangled W space that SQ-GAN quantizes
- **DiffAugment** (Zhao et al., NeurIPS 2020) augments both real and fake samples with differentiable transformations; SQ-GAN takes a complementary latent-space approach
- **Improved Consistency Regularization** (Zhao et al., 2021) proposed the latent CR that SQ-GAN directly builds upon and improves through quantization
- **LeCam Regularization** (Tseng et al., 2021) uses exponential moving averages for discriminator stabilization
- **FQ-GAN** (Yang Zhao et al., ICML 2020) applied feature quantization to the **discriminator**; SQ-GAN applies style quantization to the **generator** — a fundamentally different approach
- **VQ-GAN** (Esser et al., CVPR 2021) quantizes encoder features for image tokenization/autoregressive generation; SQ-GAN quantizes the style space for limited-data GAN training — different problem and mechanism
- **MS³D** (ICML 2024, same team) addresses the same problem from the discriminator/gradient perspective using renormalization group theory; SQ-GAN is the complementary generator-side solution

## 8. MS³D to SQ-GAN: a two-paper research program

**MS³D (Multi-Scale Structural Self-Dissimilarity)** was published at **ICML 2024** by **Jian Wang, Xin Lan, Yuxin Tian, Jiancheng Lv** — the same core team minus Jizhe Zhou. Posted to arXiv on **August 20, 2024**. MS³D observes that under limited data, the discriminator's gradient field becomes increasingly aggregated, and proposes RG (renormalization group) flow-based regularization to maintain consistent gradient patterns across scales.

**The strategic pairing is deliberate:** MS³D regularizes the discriminator side; SQ-GAN restructures the generator side. Together, they form a comprehensive attack on the limited-data GAN problem from both directions. The fact that the same core team produced both papers at consecutive top venues (ICML 2024 → CVPR 2025) reveals a sustained, strategic research agenda.

## 10. Why data-efficient GANs matter — and why this team pursues them

Data-efficient GAN training addresses a fundamental tension: GANs produce spectacular results but traditionally require tens of thousands of training images. Several factors drive research in this area.

**Medical imaging** is perhaps the strongest motivator — rare disease images, pathological slides, and specialized scans are inherently scarce and expensive to annotate. The SQ-GAN paper itself experiments on medical datasets. **Privacy regulations** — including China's **Personal Information Protection Law (PIPL, enacted November 2021)**, which restricts collection and processing of biometric and medical data — create additional incentives for methods that generate synthetic data from minimal real samples. Art, cultural heritage, and rare data domains similarly benefit. Copyright concerns around training data further motivate data-minimization approaches.

**Sichuan University's strength** in medical AI (both MachineILab and DICALab have active medical imaging projects) makes limited-data generative modeling a natural priority. The university's CS department ranks **22nd in China** and **157th globally** per EduRank.

The field exploded in 2020 with three concurrent landmark papers (StyleGAN2-ADA, DiffAugment, and early CR methods) and has grown steadily since. CVPR 2025 alone features at least two data-efficient GAN papers.

## Conclusion: a well-organized lab system producing complementary advances

SQ-GAN is not an isolated paper but the **latest output of a deliberately structured research program**. The same core team (Wang, Lan, Tian, Lv) first tackled limited-data GAN training from the discriminator side with MS³D at ICML 2024, then from the generator/latent-space side with SQ-GAN at CVPR 2025. Jizhe Zhou's addition to SQ-GAN brought multimedia forensics expertise that likely strengthened the discriminator robustness analysis.

The intellectual DNA traces from Zhang Yi's neural network theory (MachineILab, est. 2008) through Jiancheng Lv's broader AI empire (DICALab, dean of CS), down to the younger generation executing focused research on specific problems. The team's fusion of ideas — VQ-VAE-style codebook learning from the representation learning community, CLIP alignment from the vision-language community, optimal transport from mathematical optimization, and consistency regularization from the GAN training community — reflects a group that reads broadly and synthesizes deliberately.

What makes this team distinctive is not any single breakthrough but the **systematic way** they decompose a hard problem (limited-data GANs) into complementary sub-problems, assign them to specific papers, and publish them at top venues in rapid succession. For a course on deep unsupervised learning, SQ-GAN exemplifies how modern generative modeling research combines multiple technical traditions — adversarial training, discrete representation learning, foundation model knowledge transfer, and optimal transport theory — to solve practical constraints that matter in real applications from medical imaging to privacy-preserving data synthesis.
